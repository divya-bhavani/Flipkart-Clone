EMI on Debit Cards
EMI on Debit Cards feature offers customers the convenience of buying expensive items without the need for a credit card. This facility allows eligible debit card holders—typically from banks like HDFC, SBI, Axis Bank, ICICI, and Kotak Mahindra—to convert purchases into affordable monthly installments. It’s especially helpful for customers who prefer not to use or don’t own a credit card. The process is seamless: during checkout, eligible users can see the EMI option automatically and select a suitable tenure (usually 3, 6, or 9 months). With minimal documentation and zero or low interest in many cases, EMI on debit cards makes premium products more accessible to a wider audience.

Mobile Exchange offers:
At delivery, a field agent will inspect the old phone using diagnostic tools—checking for physical damage, display quality, and overall functionality. If everything checks out, the exchange value will be deducted from your final payment. For example, the iPhone 13 currently gets up to ₹27,000 off when exchanged for an iPhone 15, while a Samsung Galaxy S20 may fetch around ₹10,000–₹12,000 depending on condition. Mid-range phones like the Realme Narzo 50A or Redmi Note 10 typically offer ₹2,000–₹5,000 in exchange value. However, actual value may differ at the time of delivery based on wear, screen scratches, or hardware issues. To maximize your benefit, ensure your phone is in good condition, factory reset, and fully charged at pickup. Flipkart often pairs exchange bonuses with card discounts, making high-end phones more affordable—especially during Big Billion Days or festive sales.
